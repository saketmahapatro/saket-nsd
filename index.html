Total Mission Chronology

1941: The Genesis

Real World: Joe Simon and Jack Kirby create Steve Rogers for Timely Comics.

Fiction: Steve Rogers receives the Super Soldier Serum in a secret Brooklyn bunker. Dr. Abraham Erskine is assassinated, leaving Steve as the sole successful subject.

1942 - 1944: The WWII Theater

Rogers leads the Howling Commandos across Europe. Key battles include the rescue of Bucky Barnes at the Hydra research facility and the destruction of Baron Zemo's sonic weaponry.

1945: The Frozen Sleep

Steve Rogers stops Red Skull's "Valkyrie" bomber from destroying New York. The plane crashes in the Arctic. Rogers enters a state of cryogenic stasis.

1964: Silver Age Rebirth

In the comics (Earth-616), Rogers is found by the Avengers. He becomes the field leader of the team, adapting to a world without his 1940s peers.

2011: MCU Awakening

In the cinematic timeline, S.H.I.E.L.D. recovers Rogers. He awakens in modern-day Times Square, eventually leading the Avengers in the 2012 Battle of New York.

2014 - 2018: Civil War & Exile

The fall of S.H.I.E.L.D. (Hydra infiltration). The Sokovia Accords divide the Avengers. Steve Rogers operates underground as "Nomad" until the arrival of Thanos.

2019 - 2023: The Endgame

Half of all life is erased. Rogers leads a support group for survivors. In 2023, he returns to the past to return the Infinity Stones, retiring to live a life with Peggy Carter.

2024 - 2025: The Wings of Liberty

Sam Wilson accepts the Shield. He defeats the Flag Smashers and later thwarts President Thaddeus "Thunderbolt" Ross's attempt to weaponize Red Hulk biology.

2026: Present Day

As of Jan 10, 2026, Sam Wilson leads a new roster of Avengers. A multiversal rift confirmed in trailer leaks suggests a 2026 meeting between Sam and an elderly Steve Rogers variant.

Shield Laboratory Specifications

Chemical Composition

Proto-Adamantium (Comics): An accidental alloy of Vibranium, Steel, and an unknown catalyst created by Myron MacLain. It has never been duplicated.

Pure Vibranium (MCU): Howard Stark forged the shield using the entirety of the U.S. government's Vibranium supply in 1943. It absorbs 100% of all kinetic energy.

Aerodynamic Data

Ricochet Geometry: The shield's concave shape acts as an airfoil. It creates its own lift, allowing it to maintain speed even after bouncing off multiple surfaces.

Balance: The center of gravity is exactly in the middle of the 30-inch diameter, allowing it to be thrown as a spinning blade or a blunt disc.
